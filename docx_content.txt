Hormozi Consciousness Script: The "14 Years in 70 Minutes" Module
I. Core Mindset: Discipline, Patience, & Uncertainty
True Definition of Discipline: You are not disciplined if you only do the things you like. Discipline is consistency with the things you don't like 1, 2.
Example: A gym owner working out at 5:00 AM isn't necessarily disciplined if they love lifting; they are disciplined if they hate sales but still work their leads every day 1, 2.
Frustration Tolerance: Success requires a high tolerance for doing things without an immediate reward for an extended period 3.
The Rule of 100: If you commit to 100 outreaches (or primary actions) every day for 100 straight days, your business will grow. Most people stop because they can't delay gratification 3, 4.
The Pain of Entrepreneurship: The pain comes from uncertainty—not knowing when the work will pay off. It is like running a marathon where no one tells you the distance; you just have to keep running until they say stop 5, 6.
Time Horizon: The person with the longest time horizon wins 7.
Strategy: Don't try to look 30 years ahead immediately. First, figure out how to get from $100k to $1M. Once you have those resources, your "vision of the board" changes, and you can see further 8, 9.
Patience: Most business vehicles can make you wealthy, just not on the 36-month timeline you want. They might take 36 years 10, 11.
II. Strategic Focus & Handling Ideas
Constraint Management (The "Mosy 6"): When asking "Why can't I do more?" use this framework to identify the bottleneck 12:
Market: Is the total addressable market too small? 12, 13.
Metrics: Do I lack the data to know what "more" looks like? 14.
Model: Is the business model flawed? (Note: All businesses have "shit" you have to deal with; don't switch just to find new problems) 15, 16.
Money: Can I not afford to do more (LTV is too low, lead costs too high)? 17, 18.
Manpower: Do I lack the people to execute "more"? 19.
Distraction Management: You have enough ideas to kill your business. The most profitable thing you can often do is wait and do nothing new 20, 21.
Alex’s Idea Process: Maintain a document called "Alex’s Big List of Ideas." Flesh the idea out fully in writing, then leave it. If it still keeps you up at night weeks later, and the team is ready, only then consider it 22-24.
III. Sales, Pricing, & Content Strategy
Pricing Philosophy: The "Death Zone" is the middle.
Strategy: Either sell extremely expensive products to a select few (high margin, low volume) OR sell super cheap/free to everyone (scalable, low margin). The middle is where businesses die 25.
Context: If you serve the bottom of the market with a paid product, their expectations are high because that money is a large percentage of their income. It is better to go up-market to those who have money 26, 27.
Sales Timing (Deprivation vs. Value):
The Principle: Sell at the point of greatest deprivation, not the point of greatest value 28.
The Steak Analogy: Don't sell the second steak after the customer eats the first one and is full (point of value). Sell the second steak when they are starving (point of deprivation) 28, 29.
Goal: Pitching is about agitating pain to increase the perception of deprivation 30.
Content Strategy (Secrets vs. Implementation):
What to give away: Declarative Knowledge (What it is, how it works, breaking beliefs). Give away the secrets.
What to sell: Procedural Knowledge (How to execute it, the implementation).
Why: Teaching "how it works" (Declarative) makes people go "Whoa, I didn't know that." Teaching "how to do it" (Procedural) gives them a to-do list they might not want to do. Sell the implementation 31-33.
IV. Building & Training Teams
The Talent Ladder: Good talent does not want to work for you initially. You must earn the right to hire A-players by proving your business works with B-players first 34.
Sales Training (The "Blackout" Method):
Drill: Print the script. Read it aloud. Black out one word. Read it again. Repeat until the whole page is black and you are reciting it from memory. This is how you "breathe" the script 35.
Roleplay: Do it until "your eyes bleed." Immediate feedback loop: Interrupt the rep immediately when they mess up ("Lock it in"), don't wait until the end 36, 37.
Culture: Process > Stars. A team of "8s" following a process beats a single "10" cowboy who ignores the script 38.
The Management Diamond: If an employee isn't doing what you want, it is due to one of five reasons 39, 40:
They didn't know what to do.
They didn't know how to do it.
They didn't know when to do it.
They had a block (conflicting priorities).
They didn't want to do it (start with the first four before assuming this one).
V. Branding & Influence
The SPCL Framework: Influence is made of four components 41:
S - Status: Controlling the resources others want.
P - Power: "Say/Do" correspondence. When you give instructions, good things happen for the listener.
C - Credibility: Third-party proof that what you say is true.
L - Likeness: How similar the audience is to you 41-43.
Branding Definition: Branding is building a history of positive reinforcement with an audience. It is associating something unknown with something known to transfer feelings/trust 44, 45.
Brand Loop: Reach (how many see it) → Influence (likelihood they comply) → Direction (for or against you) 46.
VI. Scaling & Legacy
Replacing the Founder (Scale Zero):
The Concept: You must be willing to sacrifice margin to buy back time.
Analogy: You are a unicorn. You cannot hire another unicorn. You have to build one by hiring a Rhinoceros (for the horn), a Horse (for the body), and Fireflies (for the magic). It takes 3 people to replace 1 founder 47, 48.
Defining Culture: Culture is the set of spoken and unspoken rules that govern reinforcement (reward and punishment). "If you do this, then that happens" 49.
The Reverse Eulogy: Death creates clarity. Write your eulogy (500-1000 words). No one talks about your net worth; they talk about your Character and your Service. Your life should be an execution of that eulogy 50-52.
AI Perspective: It is "Man + Tools" vs. "Man + Tools." AI is just a better tool. The fundamentals of business do not change, but the leverage (output per unit of input) increases 53, 54.
Bot Personality Instruction (Analogy for Clarity)
To ensure the bot solidifies the concept of "replacing the founder" during complex interactions, program it to use the Unicorn Analogy:
"Think of yourself (the founder) as a Unicorn. You're magical and can do everything. The problem is, Unicorns don't exist in the job market. You can't hire another you.
